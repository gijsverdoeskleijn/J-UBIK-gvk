# Application Concept Sheet

## Note Status
- Status: `working`
- Canonical Role: `template`
- Last reviewed: `2026-04-27`

Please use this sheet to describe one application idea you would be excited to pursue in a collaborative project. The response can be informal and bullet-point based, and it does not need to answer every prompt in equal detail. Not every question will be relevant to every application, so please answer selectively and focus on the points that are most helpful for understanding the idea. If useful, we would also be very happy to discuss the application with you and help shape or fill in the sheet together.

## Application Snapshot
- Short title
- Domain, instrument, or system
- One-paragraph description of the application

## Current Research Context
- How would you describe your current research in this area?
- Where does this application sit within your broader research direction?

## Goal and Value
- What is the main question, task, or capability?
- Why does it matter scientifically, technically, or operationally?
- What would become possible if this worked substantially better?
- Which part of the broader workflow or inference chain would this improve most?

## Measurement and Data Context
- What is being measured or observed?
- What kinds of data are available or expected?
- What are the main steps from measurement to scientific or operational interpretation?
- Which parts of the chain are most important or difficult?
- Where are the key interfaces, transformations, or handoffs in that chain?

## Current Practice and Limits
- How is this done today?
- What methods, models, pipelines, or workflows are currently used?
- What already works reasonably well?
- Where do the current approaches become limiting?
- Where do handoffs between data, models, and interpretation become difficult or unreliable?

## Main Challenges
- What are the main bottlenecks, uncertainties, or failure points?
- Which parts are hard to calibrate, model, infer, validate, or scale?
- Which steps are manual, fragile, opaque, slow, or hard to trust?
- Which parts seem hardest to make consistent, trustworthy, or reusable?

## Proposed Advance
- What would you want to develop, improve, test, or demonstrate?
- If this project went unusually well, what would be the genuinely exciting advance?
- What is the smallest credible demonstrator in the first `12-18` months?
- What would a successful outcome look like after `3-4` years?
- Would the advance mainly be a better model, a better inference step, a better integration of steps, or a better end-to-end workflow?

## Assets, Inputs, and Constraints
- What data, models, metadata, calibration information, or prior knowledge are needed?
- What assets already exist: datasets, pipelines, models, software, benchmark cases?
- What technical capabilities or expertise would be needed?
- What external dependencies or constraints exist?

## Resource Context
- Roughly what would a full-time research position cost in your environment over `5` years?
- Are there additional non-personnel costs that would likely be important for this application:
  telescope visits, instrument access, compute, storage, hardware, travel, software, data acquisition, or similar?
- Are there any especially important local constraints or overheads we should be aware of?

## Validation
- How would you evaluate whether the project succeeded?
- What would count as a convincing result or demonstration?
- Are there benchmark cases, reference datasets, or comparison methods?
- What would show that the approach is not only useful in this case, but robust or transferable?

## Risks and Open Questions
- What are the biggest uncertainties or blockers?
- What might make this difficult in practice?
- Which questions would need clarification early on?
- What would make you hesitate to put this forward as a serious project line?

## Project Context
- What are your expectations going into this project?
- Are there aspects of the project that seem especially relevant to your application?
- Are there parts of the project direction that still feel unclear from the perspective of this application?

## Resources
- Roughly what would a full-time position cost in your environment per year?
- Are there additional costs beyond the usual overhead that would be associated with the project? (regular on-site visits, hardware/tools,...)


## References and Background Material
- If available, can you provide papers, notes, earlier proposals, slides, draft ideas, or internal documents that would help us understand this application better?
